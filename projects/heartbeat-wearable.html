Would You Understand


Overview

How can we create intimate connections through shared physiological rhythms? This project explores haptic communication as a form of non-verbal intimacy between two people. By translating one person's heartbeat into tactile vibrations felt by another, the wearable creates a subtle, continuous connection that exists below the threshold of conscious awareness.


Technical Implementation

Hardware: Arduino Mega 2560, HC-05 Bluetooth module, custom vibrotactile actuators, soft silicone housing

Software: Smartphone camera-based rPPG (remote photoplethysmography) for contactless heart rate detection, Arduino code for motor control, Bluetooth communication

Key Challenge: Achieving consistent, continuous vibration that matches natural heartbeat rhythm (50-100 BPM). Solved by implementing a pulse interpolation system that maintains tempo between sensor readings.


Process & Iteration

Initial prototypes used direct sensor-to-motor mapping, which produced inconsistent vibrations. Through testing, I discovered that continuous tempo maintenance was crucial for creating a felt presence rather than just data transmission.

The housing went through three iterations: hard plastic (uncomfortable), fabric pouches (inconsistent contact), and finally soft skin-like silicone that maintains gentle pressure while allowing natural movement.


Outcome & Reflection

The final prototype successfully creates an intimate connection between two users, with testers reporting feelings of presence and comfort when receiving their partner's heartbeat. The device functions as a wearable that can be placed on arms, wrists, or torso.

Next Steps: Exploring multi-user networks (3+ people sharing heartbeats), investigating heart rate variability as emotional communication, and developing smaller form factors for everyday wear.
