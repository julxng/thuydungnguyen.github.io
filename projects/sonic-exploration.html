Sonic Exploration


Overview

How do we perceive and interact with invisible phenomena? This installation explores the sonification of electromagnetic fields and human presence, transforming intangible energy into tangible sound experiences. By mapping electromagnetic frequencies to auditory feedback, visitors experience the hidden energies that surround them.


Technical Implementation

Hardware: Arduino-based electromagnetic field sensors, amplified speaker arrays, proximity detection using IR and RF sensors

Software: Real-time signal processing in Pure Data (Pd), frequency modulation synthesis, gesture recognition for interactive audio control

Key Challenge: Creating responsive audio without latency. Solved through optimized sensor polling and dedicated audio processing thread.


Process & Iteration

Initial attempts using direct frequency mapping resulted in chaotic, unmusical output. The breakthrough came when I implemented harmonic scales and frequency constraints, creating aesthetically pleasing sonifications.

The installation evolved through three phases: silent electromagnetic visualization, basic sonification, and finally interactive soundscape where visitor movement directly influences the audio landscape.


Outcome & Reflection

The final installation successfully made the invisible visible and audible, creating an immersive experience that challenges perceptions of digital environments. Visitors reported heightened awareness of their technological surroundings.

Next Steps: Expanding to include biometric data sonification, collaborating with musicians for compositional frameworks, and creating portable versions for different venues.
